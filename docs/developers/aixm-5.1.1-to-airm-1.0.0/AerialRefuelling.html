AerialRefuelling

AerialRefuelling.anchor

The definition of the AerialRefuelling anchor.

Properties Definition Type
missing data A procedure used by the military to transfer fuel from one aircraft to another during flight.
name An identifier by which the aerial refuelling route is known.
type The type of aerial refuelling procedure based on its configuration.
tankerChannel The Tactical Air Navigation System (TACAN) UHF channel assigned to the tanker aircraft to provide omnidirectional course and distance navigational information during air refuelling operations.
specialRefuelling An indication that the aerial refuelling procedure supports special missions/sortie.
reverseDirectionTurn An indicator of the turn direction in an aerial manoeuvre (referred to as the Direction of Course Reversal Turn) performed by aircraft during aerial refuelling, where it can reverse its direction of course by turning around either right or left. It only applies to bi-directional routes
receiverChannel The Tactical Air Navigation System (TACAN) UHF channel assigned to an aircraft receiving fuel to provide omnidirectional course and distance navigational information during air refuelling operations.
radarBeaconSetting The setting to be used by the airborne navigation radar beacon during aerial refuel operations.
helicopterRoute An indication that the aerial refuelling procedure is designed only for helicopter usage.
protectingAirspace The airspace that protects the AerialRefuelling area.
designatorSuffix An affix placed after a route designator used to qualify an aerial refuelling route.
designatorPrefix An affix placed before a route designator used to identify an aerial refuelling route.
designatorNumber A designator that identifies the aerial refuelling route.
designatorDirection An indicator of the general cardinal direction of the aerial refuelling procedure
bidirectionalUse An indication that the aerial refuel track supports simultaneous opposite direction aerial refuelling
xbandRadarSetting The setting to be used by the Airborne Identification Radar beacon during aerial refuel operations.
annotation
availability The operational availability of the AerialRefuelling.
managingOrganisation The OrganisationAuthority that manages the AerialRefuelling.
track The definition of the AerialRefuellingTrack
oppositeTrack The opposite AerialRefuelling.
anchor The definition of the AerialRefuelling anchor.
